💡 Marketing That Changed Culture

💎
"A Diamond is Forever"
The slogan that linked diamonds to eternal love and marriage. Launched in 1947, it became the most successful advertising campaign of the 20th century.
💰
Two Months' Salary Rule
Started as 1 month (1930s), then 2 months (1980s), even 3 months in Japan. De Beers literally set the spending standard for engagement rings worldwide.
⛏️
Artificial Scarcity
Controlled supply through African mining monopoly and managing a small number of large global buyers. Created perception of rarity for a relatively common mineral.

⚖️ Law of One Price in Action
The diamond market is now commoditized due to lab-grown alternatives. Diamonds are small and easy to transport, so lower Chinese production costs translate immediately to lower prices worldwide in India, London, and New York.

💎 The Premium Problem
Natural diamonds still command a ~5x premium over lab-grown stones at retail, but institutional investors don't believe De Beers can capture that premium much longer. Even their own accountants are slashing valuations.

De Beers production down 22% in 2024. Guidance for 2025: 20-23 million carats (down from 30-33M). The empire is shrinking fast.
