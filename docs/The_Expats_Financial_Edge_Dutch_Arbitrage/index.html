Foreign Asset Tax Shield

For expats with rulings granted before 2024, the "partial non-resident status" provides a powerful advantage over locals.

Benefit:

  • Effectively shields global assets from the Dutch Box 3 wealth tax.
  • No tax on foreign savings accounts.
  • No tax on foreign investment portfolios.
  • No tax on property owned outside the Netherlands.

*Note: This specific benefit is being phased out, making it a critical, time-sensitive advantage for existing beneficiaries.

The Strategic Arbitrage Toolkit

Dutch tax code contains several schemes that, if used strategically, create low-competition business advantages by legally optimizing VAT.

1. Travel Agency Scheme (TOMS)

When organizing trips *outside* the EU, you only pay VAT on your profit margin, and that margin VAT is 0%.

On a €12,000 sale, the €2,000 profit margin has a 0% VAT, maximizing high-margin travel businesses.

2. B2B Reverse Charge VAT

For B2B services to clients inside the EU (but outside NL), you invoice at 0% VAT. The client handles VAT in their own country.

1 You (NL)
2 Invoice at 0% VAT
3 B2B Client (EU)
4 Client pays VAT locally

Result: No VAT collection, better cash flow, and cleaner accounting.

3. KOR (Small Business Scheme)

For any business with turnover under the annual threshold, you can opt out of VAT entirely.

€20,000

Annual Turnover Threshold

  • You do not charge VAT to clients.
  • You do not file VAT returns.
  • You cannot reclaim VAT on expenses.

Ideal for service side-hustles (coaching, tutoring) where clients prefer lower, simpler pricing.

Housing: Subletting Rules

Landlords often cannot "unreasonably refuse" a request to sublet part of your apartment, even if the contract requires permission.

The Strategy:

  1. Rent a 2 or 3-bedroom apartment.
  2. Formally request to sublet one room.
  3. Sublet at market rate (not to tourists).
  4. Use the sublet income to cover most or all of your own rent, effectively living rent-free.
