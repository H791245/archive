Solution #1: Use B-L-U-F (Bottom Line Up Front)

Present ONE point, clearly and concisely

  • Keep it to 7-10 seconds maximum
  • One point only, not 2 or 3 jammed together
  • Reframe the question in your answer
  • Pause and look UP, not down
Question:
"How did you get into communication coaching?"
Answer (B-L-U-F):
"Well, what got me into communication coaching was a personal struggle with speaking myself."

Solution #2: Use P.R.E.P Framework

Structure your response with 4 simple components

P: Point
R: Reason
E: Example
P: Point
  • Point: Your BLUF (the one main thing)
  • Reason: Why you said that (first thing that comes to mind)
  • Example: Tell a story or specific instance
  • Point: Restate your main point to reinforce
Question:
"What is your ultimate goal with your coaching business?"
P.R.E.P Response:
Point: "To help as many professionals as possible get confident when put on the spot."

Reason: "Because I struggled so much with this myself."

Example: "When preparing for interviews, I drill on five key questions."

Point: "That's why I'm so passionate about this mission."

Solution #3: Lower the Stakes - Relive, Don't Report

Talk naturally like you're having coffee with a friend

  • Replay specific scenes from your own experience
  • Narrate it as you see it, not in technical terms
  • Let it flow naturally instead of trying to sound impressive
  • Use personal stories that adapt to any question
Question:
"What is your strategy for preparing for interviews?"
Relive, Don't Report:
"Get 8 personal stories down on paper that you can adapt to almost any interviewing question. When I was preparing for my current job, I remember drilling on five key questions about their personal values..."
