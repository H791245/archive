Professor Jensen

"Teach 90% of the time"

Spends majority of time teaching organization. Creates 'Vulcan mind meld' where unrelated employees say same things.

If you're not spending 90% of your time teaching, you're not doing your job. This creates organizational alignment at scale.

Complacency Kills

"We're 30 days from bankruptcy"

Maintained urgency mentality for 31 years. Told employees monthly 'we're 30 days from going out of business' even when successful.

Only the paranoid survive. This constant urgency prevents complacency and drives continuous innovation.

üí™

Tortured into Greatness

"I look in mirror and say you suck"

Habit of self-criticism. Excellence is capacity to take pain. 'I hope suffering happens to you' - resilience matters more than intelligence.

Greatness comes from character. Character is formed from people who suffered. This builds resilience and mental toughness.

üöÄ

No Rearview Mirror

"Do great things, then do more"

Never dwell on past success. Make something great then do it again. Constant reinvention is mandatory, not optional.

Past success can become a trap. Always look forward and continuously reinvent to stay ahead of the competition.

Communication & Transparency

üìù

Whiteboard Only

"No PowerPoints allowed"

Primary form of communication. Forces rigorous, transparent thinking. No hiding incomplete ideas. Must demonstrate thought process in real-time.

Whiteboard represents possibility and ephemerality. It forces clear thinking and prevents hiding behind polished presentations.

üîä

Public Criticism

"Everyone learns from mistakes"

Criticizes publicly so entire company learns. 'We're optimizing for company learning, not for not embarrassing somebody.'

Whole organization benefits from single mistake. This maximizes learning efficiency across the entire company.

üí¨

Blunt & Direct

"Short, clear, memorable"

Napoleon-style high coup communication. Short emails, clear direction. Easy to understand means easy to spread.

No long-winded explanations. Clear communication scales better and reduces misunderstandings across the organization.

üéØ

LUAA

"Listen-Understand-Answer-Answer"

Warning when someone rambles. Stop, listen to question, understand question, answer question. Forces clarity in all communication.

This framework ensures efficient communication and prevents wasted time on unclear or misunderstood exchanges.

Flat Organization

"60 direct reports, no 1:1s"

Maintains flat structure for speed. Information travels at speed of light. No bureaucracy. Weeds out people who can't think independently.

Fast decision making and unfiltered information flow. This structure forces everyone to be self-sufficient and accountable.

Top Five Emails

"Reads 100+ daily"

Employees send 5 bullet points on what they're working on. Intercepts weak signals before they become problems.

Unfiltered information from entire company. Drinks scotch and reads emails to stay connected to every part of the business.

Mission is Boss

"Pilot in Command owns it"

Every project has accountable person - a Pilot in Command. Everything has person's name attached. No hiding behind departments.

Ultimate boss is the mission itself. Clear accountability prevents diffusion of responsibility and ensures ownership.

üìö

Go to School on Everyone

"CEO shows up to learn"

Constant learning. Shows up personally to academic conferences. 'I'm here to learn.' Recalled biographical details of 50 new hires at party.

In the details. Personal attention to learning and people creates deep organizational knowledge and connection.

Execution & Speed

Speed of Light

"Benchmark: physically possible"

Only benchmark is maximum possible speed. Break projects into tasks with zero delays, zero queues. Judge against speed of light, not competitors.

Don't compare to what you used to do or what competitors do. Compare to the theoretical maximum speed possible.

üî•

Unapologetically Extreme

"No one will outwork me"

Works every day. If not working, thinking about work. 'Working is relaxing for me.' Second place is first loser. Will grill you at the urinal.

Extreme dedication and work ethic. This sets the standard for the entire organization's commitment level.

Strategy is Action

"No 5-year plans"

Strategy is not words, strategy is actions. Continuous planning, no periodic planning system. World is living, breathing thing. Steer the boat each day.

Adapt continuously rather than following rigid long-term plans. The market changes too fast for static strategies.

ü•á

Highest Priority First

"Day succeeds before arriving"

Clear priority list. Completes most important work first thing. 'Before I even get to work, my day is already a success.' Ruthlessly prioritize.

Focus on handful of critical things. Early completion of high-priority items ensures daily success regardless of interruptions.

Strategy & Innovation

üêÑ

Ship the Whole Cow

"Use every part"

Rejected chips sold at lower price points. Defend against low-end competitors. Use almost every part like butchers. Turn nothing into revenue without expensive R&D.

Maximum efficiency by finding markets for every product tier. This blocks competitors and maximizes return on R&D investment.

üåü

Create Markets

"Zero billion dollar markets"

Build where there are no customers and no competitors yet. 'We decided to build something where we are sure there are no customers' - no customers means no competitors.

Create markets, don't fight for share. Building new markets gives you first-mover advantage and defines the rules.

üí∞

Choke with Gold

"Reward greatness immediately"

Special stock grants for extraordinary contributions. Pours over allocation reports. Can reach down anytime and award stock directly. Make people feel appreciated in the moment.

Immediate recognition prevents talent loss and reinforces behaviors that drive exceptional performance.

üåä

Swarm Greatest Opportunity

"20-year CUDA bet paid off"

2006 CUDA launch - invested heavily despite 45% to 35% margin drop. 'Deep learning is going to be really big. We should go all in.' Educated the market.

Overruled skeptical executives. 20-year bet paid off. Sometimes you must make massive bets on transformative opportunities.
