Short, friendly guide that explains what the benefit is, how to use it, and what good things (and things to watch) happen.

What is this? (Imagine it like a helpful swap)

Instead of paying taxes on some of your salary and then buying a gym membership or a bike with the rest, you tell the company: "Please use some of my salary to pay the gym for me before tax." The company does that and you pay less tax ‚Äî so you save money.

How to use it ‚Äî 4 easy steps
1

Go to MyBenefits (the company site where you pick benefits).

2

Pick a wellbeing thing you want (example: sports, bike, desk chair, health check).

3

Choose to pay using the Wellbeing Fiscal Arrangement ‚Äî that means part of your gross salary or extra holiday days will be used.

4

Company gives you that item or pays the provider, and your taxes are lower ‚Äî you keep more money overall.

FAQ ‚Äî short and clear
Is this free?
You still pay, but you pay less tax.
‚û§
Not exactly free. You use part of your salary or holidays to pay for the item, but because it is handled before tax you pay less overall. That saves you money compared to buying it after taxes.
Can I use it for everything?
Only for certain wellbeing items listed by the company.
‚û§
You can use it only for things in the MyBenefits list (sports, bikes, health checks, etc.). Check "Benefits at a Glance" in the guide or MyBenefits portal.
What happens if I don't use it?
Unused yearly budget expires.
‚û§
Each year the company sets a limit for how much you can use. If you don't use the whole amount, it does not carry over ‚Äî it disappears at year end.
Can I change my mind?
Usually not after you confirm ‚Äî unless something big changes.
‚û§
You usually can't change your choice after the enrollment period except if you have a qualifying status change (like moving, having a baby, etc.).
Want to know more?
Ask Colleague Service Center or open MyBenefits to see exact amounts and the yearly budget your company set.
Bottom line
This arrangement helps you pay less tax when buying health & wellbeing things via the company. It can save you money, but think about how it affects other things like future benefits based on your taxed salary.
