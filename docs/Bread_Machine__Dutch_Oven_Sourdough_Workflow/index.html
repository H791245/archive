First Rise (Bulk Fermentation)
2-6 hours (or overnight)
This is your first major control point for flavor. Two paths available:
  • Fast path: Room temp, 2-3 hours (good flavor)
  • Flavor path: Fridge overnight 6-24 hours (excellent tang)
  • Keep machine lid closed during fermentation
  • Dough should be noticeably puffier (doesn't need to double)

10 minutes
Gently remove dough and shape it into a round. Minimal handling preserves gas bubbles (they create the open crumb).
  • Remove dough from machine onto floured surface
  • Gently pre-shape into a round (don't punch down)
  • Line Dutch oven with parchment paper
  • Place shaped dough in parchment-lined Dutch oven

4 hours at room temp OR 6-12 hours cold
Let shaped dough rise again inside the covered Dutch oven. Cold proofing overnight = maximum flavor.
  • Cover Dutch oven with its lid
  • Room temp: Rise 4 hours until ~double size
  • Cold: Place in fridge 6-12 hours overnight
  • Check if dough springs back slowly when poked (means ready)

Pro Tips for Success

üå°Ô∏è Temperature Control
Cold fermentation (overnight fridge) develops superior tang compared to room temperature. This is the secret to professional-tasting sourdough.
üí® Steam is Essential
Never open the oven during the first 25 minutes with the lid on. Steam escaping = soft crust. The trapped steam is what creates the crispy exterior.
‚è±Ô∏è Don't Rush Cooling
Minimum 1 hour cooling. The internal steam continues cooking the crumb during this time. Cutting early creates a gummy interior.
üéØ Starter Readiness
Your starter must be active and bubbly (fed 4-8 hours prior). Dead starter = dense bread. A reliable indicator: it should double in size after feeding.
üìè Dough Consistency
After kneading, dough should be slightly sticky but hold its shape. If too dry, add 1 tbsp water. Too wet? Add 1 tbsp flour and restart cycle.
üçû Scoring Optional
Scoring (slashing the top) is aesthetic and helps control expansion. Not required for taste, but it creates the signature "ear" and professional appearance.
