Why It Was Needed

Learning from Singapore's painful past

July 21, 1964

First Race Riots

23 deaths 454 injuries

Triggered during Muslim procession celebrating Prophet Muhammad's birthday. Violence lasted 17 days.

September 2, 1964

Second Race Riots

13 deaths 106 injuries

Sparked by killing of Malay trishaw rider in Geylang Serai.

1980s

Ethnic Enclaves Re-forming

Bedok/Tampines: >30% Malay households
Hougang: >90% Chinese households

Despite earlier integration efforts, ethnic clustering was returning.

March 1989

EIP Implementation

Minister S. Dhanabalan introduced the policy to prevent "return to the pre-independence days when the various races lived in separate areas."

How It Works

The mechanics of ethnic balance

1

Quota Setting

Ethnic quotas set for each block and neighbourhood based on national demographics

"The Intrusive Part"

This constrains individual choice - you can't just buy where you want. The government decides ethnic composition of each block and precinct. It "doesn't come comfortably to the liberal mind."
