🩺 Hydronephrosis & Kidney Stone Surgery: Complete Guide

Comprehensive Information on Diagnosis, Treatment, and Prevention

🔍 Understanding Hydronephrosis

Hydronephrosis is kidney swelling caused by urine backup, often due to kidney stones or other obstructions that prevent proper drainage.

🎯

Common Causes

  • 💎 Kidney stones (most common)
  • 🔄 Enlarged prostate
  • 🤱 Pregnancy
  • 🦠 Urinary tract infections
  • 🩸 Blood clots
  • 🎗️ Tumors or cancer
  • 🚫 Ureteral strictures
⚠️

Symptoms

  • 🔥 Flank or back pain
  • 🚻 Frequent urination
  • ⚡ Increased urge to urinate
  • 💧 Pain during urination
  • 🌡️ Fever (if infected)
  • 🤢 Nausea and vomiting
  • ❌ Incomplete bladder emptying
💊

Treatment Options

  • 🩺 Catheter drainage
  • 🔧 Nephrostomy tube
  • ⚕️ Surgery
  • 💉 Antibiotics
  • 🔗 DJ stent placement
  • 💊 Medications

💎 Types of Kidney Stones

Understanding different stone types helps guide prevention strategies and treatment approaches.

💙

Calcium Oxalate

61%
Causes: High oxalate foods
Avoid: Spinach, chocolate, nuts, beets, tea
Recurrence: 38%
🔷

Calcium Phosphate

15%
Causes: High urine pH (alkaline)
Common in: Women (2:1 ratio)
Recurrence: 43%
❤️

Uric Acid

10-15%
Causes: High protein diet, gout, diabetes
Avoid: Red meat, organ meats, seafood
Recurrence: 51%
🧡

Struvite

5-15%
Causes: UTIs, infections
Common in: Women (3:1 ratio)
Recurrence: 41%
💜

Cystine

1-2%
Causes: Genetic disorder (cystinuria)
Age: Usually 0-20 years
Recurrence: 89% (highest)

⚕️ Stone Removal Surgery Options

Three main surgical approaches are available depending on stone size, location, and patient factors.

⚡

ESWL (Shock Wave Lithotripsy)

External shock waves break stones into smaller fragments that can pass naturally.

Best For Stones <2 cm, softer stones
Recovery 1-2 days, outpatient
Anesthesia Sedation or general
Complications Hematuria, flank pain, ureteral blockage, kidney hematoma (<1%), UTI
🔍

Ureteroscopy (URS)

Scope inserted through urethra to directly remove or break stones with laser.

Best For Stones in ureter or kidney
Recovery 1-2 days
Anesthesia General
Complications (<20%) Ureteral perforation, bleeding, stone migration (9-12%), stricture (0.3-4.4%), infection/sepsis
🔧

PCNL (Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy)

Small incision in back provides direct access to kidney for large stone removal.

Best For Large stones >2 cm, staghorn stones
Recovery 2-4 weeks, 2-4 day hospital stay
Anesthesia General
Complications Bleeding (may need transfusion), infection/sepsis, organ injury, pneumothorax, prolonged urine leakage

🔗 DJ (Double-J) Ureteral Stent

A temporary tube placed to keep the ureter open and allow proper urine drainage after stone procedures.

🎯

Purpose

  • Keeps ureter open after surgery
  • Allows urine to drain properly
  • Helps resolve post-surgical edema
  • Prevents obstruction
  • Placed after stone procedures
😣

Common Side Effects (>80%)

  • Frequent urination
  • Urgent need to urinate
  • Burning during urination
  • Blood in urine (hematuria)
  • Flank pain (19.3%)
  • Bladder spasms
  • Lower back/abdominal pain
⚠️

Serious Complications

  • Stent migration
  • Encrustation (9-76% if prolonged)
  • Stent occlusion
  • Fragmentation
  • UTI/urosepsis
  • Stone formation on stent
  • Kidney damage if forgotten

⏰ Stent Timeline

Placement
Immediately after stone surgery
Duration
Few days to 6 months
(typical: 6 weeks)
Removal
15 min procedure via cystoscope or string pull
⚠️ CRITICAL: Never Forget Your Stent!
Encrustation rate: 9.2% if <6 weeks → 47.5% at 6-12 weeks → 76.3% if >12 weeks
💊

Medications for Stent Discomfort

Alpha Blockers Tamsulosin - Relax bladder/ureter muscles
NSAIDs Ibuprofen - Reduce inflammation and pain
Anticholinergics Oxybutynin - Prevent bladder spasms
Phenazopyridine Pyridium - Relieves painful urination

🍎 Prevention - Diet & Lifestyle

Lifestyle modifications and dietary changes can significantly reduce your risk of developing kidney stones.

✅ DO

💧 Hydration (MOST IMPORTANT)
2.5-3 liters (12 glasses) daily
🍊 Citrus Fruits
Lemons, oranges, grapefruit (increase citrate)
🥛 Calcium-Rich Foods
Milk, yogurt, cheese (prevents oxalate absorption)
🍎 Fruits & Vegetables
High in citrate, natural stone inhibitors
🌱 Plant Proteins
Beans, lentils, tofu, quinoa
☀️ Vitamin D
Fatty fish, eggs, fortified foods
🏃 Regular Exercise
30 min moderate activity, 3-5x/week
⚖️ Maintain Healthy Weight
Reduces stone formation risk

❌ DON'T

🧂 High Sodium
Processed foods, canned soups, fast food
🥩 Excessive Animal Protein
Red meat, organ meats, pork (increases uric acid)
🥬 High-Oxalate Foods
Spinach, chocolate, nuts, beets, tea, rhubarb
🥤 Cola Drinks
High in phosphate (promotes stones)
🍬 Added Sugars
Corn syrup, fructose, agave nectar
🍺 Alcohol
Causes dehydration
🐟 High-Purine Seafood
Sardines, anchovies, shellfish
🛋️ Sedentary Lifestyle
Increases obesity and stone risk
🎯

Stone-Specific Diet Tips

Calcium Oxalate Pair oxalate foods with calcium; reduce sodium; limit animal protein
Uric Acid Limit animal protein; lose weight if overweight; increase alkaline foods
Struvite Treat UTIs promptly; maintain good hygiene
Cystine Drink LOTS of water (main prevention); low-sodium diet

🩹 Post-Surgery Recovery & Care

Proper recovery care and monitoring are essential for successful outcomes and preventing complications.

⏱️

Recovery Timeline

  • ESWL: 1-2 days
  • Ureteroscopy: 1-2 days to 1 week
  • PCNL: 2-4 weeks full recovery
  • Return to work: 1-2 days (light) to 2 weeks (heavy work)
💊

Pain Management

  • NSAIDs: Ibuprofen, Naproxen (most effective)
  • Acetaminophen: Tylenol
  • Alpha blockers: Tamsulosin
  • Opioids: Only if necessary (<3 days)
🚨

Warning Signs - Call Doctor

  • Fever >100.4°F (38°C)
  • Severe, persistent pain
  • Heavy bleeding
  • Inability to urinate
  • Severe nausea/vomiting
  • Signs of infection
✅

Recovery Do's

  • Drink plenty of water
  • Rest adequately
  • Take medications as prescribed
  • Attend all follow-up appointments
  • Strain urine to catch stone fragments
❌

Recovery Don'ts

  • No heavy lifting (>10 lbs) for 2 weeks
  • Avoid strenuous exercise
  • No sexual activity for 1 week
  • Don't skip follow-up imaging
  • Don't ignore pain or symptoms
📅 Follow-up Care is Critical
Imaging at 4-6 weeks • Stone analysis • 24-hour urine collection • Regular monitoring for high-risk patients
