🧪 Key Sleep Chemicals

🔬

ADENOSINE

Sleep Pressure Builder

  • Accumulates during wake
  • Cleared during sleep
  • Blocked by caffeine
🌙

MELATONIN

Sleep Signal Hormone

  • Produced in darkness
  • Peaks 2-4 AM
  • Suppressed by light
☀️

CORTISOL

Wake Hormone

  • Peaks at wake time
  • Decreases through day
  • Energizes your body

✅ The Best Way to Sleep

⏰ TIMING

  • ✓ Same bedtime & wake time daily (even weekends)
  • ✓ Aim for 10-11 PM bedtime
  • ✓ Get 7-9 hours total
  • ✓ Complete 4-6 full cycles

💡 LIGHT EXPOSURE

  • ☀️ Bright light within 30-60 min of waking
  • 🌙 Avoid bright/blue light 1-2 hours before bed
  • 🌑 Keep bedroom completely dark
  • 💡 Morning light = earlier sleep, Evening light = later sleep

🏠 ENVIRONMENT

  • ❄️ Cool room (65°F / 18°C)
  • 🔇 Dark and quiet
  • 🛏️ Comfortable mattress
  • 🪟 Consider blackout curtains

🎯 BEHAVIORS

  • ☕ No caffeine 8-10 hours before bed
  • 🍷 Limit alcohol (disrupts sleep)
  • 🏃 Exercise during day (not near bedtime)
  • 📖 Relaxing pre-sleep routine

😴 NAPPING

  • ⏰ Short naps only (20-30 min)
  • 🕐 Early afternoon only
  • ⚠️ Long/late naps hurt nighttime sleep

⚠️ What Disrupts Sleep

❌ Irregular sleep schedule
❌ Light at night
❌ Late caffeine
❌ Alcohol
❌ Screen time before bed
❌ Late meals
❌ Long/late naps
❌ Stress and anxiety

🎯 Key Takeaways

Sleep is regulated by TWO processes: Process S (homeostatic sleep pressure) and Process C (circadian rhythm)
Process S: Adenosine builds during waking (~16 hrs), creating sleep pressure. It's cleared during sleep
Process C: Your 24-hour body clock controlled by light. Melatonin rises at night, cortisol peaks in morning
Optimal sleep occurs when both processes align: high sleep pressure + circadian sleep phase
Best practices: Consistent schedule + light management + cool dark environment = quality sleep
Target: 7-9 hours total, completing 4-6 sleep cycles of 90-120 minutes each
