What Are They?

Understanding the basic science and properties of these compounds

MDMA

Chemical Name: 3,4-methylenedioxymethamphetamine
Common Names: Ecstasy, Molly, E
How It Works: Increases serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine, and oxytocin
Duration: 4-6 hours

Psilocybin

Chemical Name: Psilocybin (converts to psilocin in body)
Common Names: Magic mushrooms, Shrooms, Mushies
How It Works: Activates serotonin 5-HT2A receptors in the brain
Duration: 4-6 hours (onset: 20-40 minutes)

Risks & Cons

Important safety considerations and potential adverse effects

MDMA Risks

  • Increased heart rate and blood pressure
  • Jaw clenching (bruxism)
  • Nausea, headache, anxiety
  • Potential neurotoxicity with chronic recreational use
  • Risk of serotonin syndrome if combined with SSRIs
  • Potential for overheating and dehydration
  • Memory and cognitive issues with heavy recreational use

Psilocybin Risks

  • Bad trips - intense fear, anxiety, paranoia
  • Nausea, vomiting, stomach discomfort
  • Increased heart rate and blood pressure
  • Confusion and disorientation
  • Risk of prolonged psychosis (rare)
  • Flashbacks in some cases
  • Risk of toxic mushroom poisoning if misidentified
  • Impaired judgment during trip

MDMA Contraindications

  • Cardiovascular disease
  • Uncontrolled hypertension
  • Taking SSRI or MAO-I antidepressants
  • History of seizures

Psilocybin Contraindications

  • Schizophrenia or family history
  • Bipolar I disorder
  • History of psychosis
  • Cardiovascular disease (relative)
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding
  • Unresolved trauma without coping skills
