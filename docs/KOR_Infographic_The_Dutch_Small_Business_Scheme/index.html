The KOR is a voluntary VAT exemption. It simplifies your business administration by letting you opt-out of the VAT system entirely.

The Three Core Rules of KOR

Participating in the KOR means a fundamental shift in your VAT handling. It's a simple trade-off: you gain administrative simplicity but lose the ability to reclaim VAT.

The Benefits: Simplicity

The primary gains are administrative peace of mind and more attractive pricing for private customers (B2C).

Key Takeaway: You trade financial deductions for a significant reduction in administrative workload and complexity.

The Costs: No Deductions

The main drawback is financial: you cannot reclaim any VAT, making all your business purchases effectively more expensive.

Key Takeaway: If your business has high expenses or invests in costly equipment, this inability to reclaim VAT can be a significant cost.

Is KOR Right for You?

The KOR is not a one-size-fits-all solution. It is highly beneficial for some business models and disadvantageous for others. Your client base and expense structure are the most important factors.

The 2025 Rules: More Flexibility

As of January 1, 2025, the KOR scheme has become significantly more flexible, removing the old 3-year minimum commitment and long re-entry waiting periods.

Key Takeaway: The new rules make the KOR a much lower-risk choice. You are no longer "locked in" and can adapt to your business's growth more easily.
